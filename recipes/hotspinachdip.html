Hot Spinach Dip

Description

Picture this...it's Friday night and your three best friends drop by unannounced for an impromptu girl's night. Your first thought is probably, "This is going to be great!" But your next thought is surely, "Oh no! What am I going to serve them?!" I was in this exact situation when BAM! The thought hit me. I had all the ingredients to make a simple, but incredibly tasty, hot spinach dip. Guaranteed to be a crowd pleaser, of course!

Creamy, cheesy, contains vegetables, and you get to dip bread or crackers? Say no more! Here are some secrets to this recipe's success. First, make sure you squeeze as much water out of your spinach as possible. You can do this with your hands, a potato ricer, or squeeze the spinach between a few layers of paper towels. Second, I recommend using fresh spinach for a brighter flavor, but you can use frozen in a pinch. Third, try to use at least 2 different types of cheese to increase the depth of flavor. Finally, you can make it up to 8 hours ahead - just store it in the fridge until you're ready to bake it!

Ingredients

  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 10 ounces fresh spinach leaves
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp ground pepper
  • 1/2 cup grated parmesan cheese
  • 3/4 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • bread, crackers, and/or vegetables for serving
  • cooking spray

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees F. Coat a small baking dish or oven-safe skillet with cooking spray.
  2. Next, steam or saute the spinach until wilted. Once cool, wring out all of the excess water and chop it coarsely.
  3. Place the cream cheese, sour cream, cooked spinach, garlic, salt, pepper, parmesan cheese, and mozzarella cheese in a bowl. Stir until well combined.
  4. Transfer the mixture to your sprayed baking dish. Bake for 20 minutes.
  5. Sprinkle with chopped parsley and serve with bread, crackers, and/or vegetables.
